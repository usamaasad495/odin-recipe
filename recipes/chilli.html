Chilli

Description

Chili con carne (also spelled chilli con carne or chile con carne and shortened to chili or chilli; Spanish pronunciation: [ˈtʃili kon ˈkaɾne]), meaning "chili with meat", is a spicy stew containing chili peppers (sometimes in the form of chili powder), meat (usually beef), tomatoes and optionally kidney beans.[1] Other seasonings may include garlic, onions, and cumin. The dish originated in northern Mexico or southern Texas.[1]

  • 1 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 jalapeño, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp. tomato paste

    Steps

    1. Add tomato paste and stir to coat vegetables. Add tomatoes, beans, stock, and seasonings. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    2. Bring to a boil then reduce heat and let simmer, 30 minutes.
    3. Serve with cheese, sour cream, and coriander.
